ORDER TERMINATING SUSPENSION AND IMPOSING PROBATION

PROCEDURAL AND FACTUAL HISTORY

On November 22, 2023, Disciplinary Panel A (“Panel A” or the “Panel”) of the
Maryland State Board of Physicians (“the Board”) summarily suspended the Maryland
medical license of Daniel D. Dietrick, M.D, On January 11, 2024, the Panel affirmed the
summary suspension. On April 3, 2024, Dr. Dietrick and the Panel entered into a Consent
Order. Under the terms of the Consent Order, Panel A suspended Dr. Dietrick’s medical
license for a minimum period of nine months, required Dr. Dietrick to complete a course
in professionalism and required Dr. Dietrick to enroll in the Maryland Professional
Rehabilitation Program (“MPRP”) with monitoring and quarterly reports from MPRP.'
The Consent Order provided that, after nine months, if MPRP determined that Dr. Dietrick
is safe to practice medicine and has complied with MPRP’s recommendations and
requirements, he could petition Panel A to terminate the suspension and that, if the

suspension is terminated, Panel A could impose conditions on his return to practice.

' A copy of the April 3, 2024 Consent Order is incorporated by reference and available on
request.
